Abstract

Breath as Event explores sound ontology through the concept of breath understood as an event rather than a fixed object. The work proposes an event-oriented perspective in which breathing functions as a generative structure for rhythm, voice and sonic presence. From this viewpoint sound is interpreted as a temporal phenomenon emerging from processes of breath, vibration and perceptual interaction. The booklet forms part of the research direction Sound as Event, investigating sound ontology, auditory perception and the relationship between breath, voice and acoustic phenomena.
