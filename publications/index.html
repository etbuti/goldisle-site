Abstract

Breath as Event explores sound as a temporal and embodied occurrence rather than a fixed object. The booklet examines initiation, breath, and decision structures within contemporary sound practice, combining conceptual research with artistic and sociological perspectives.

Contents

  • Prelude: Why “breath” becomes a threshold
  • Terms & orientations (brief glossary)
  • Breath as initiation: conditions under which sound may occur
  • Field notes: practice conditions outside the institution
  • Implications for the series (toward Pulse and Voice)
  • Appendix: selected bibliography (ISBN-based)
